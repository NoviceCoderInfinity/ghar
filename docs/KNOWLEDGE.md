## City blocks (inject ONE, chosen from browser geolocation → city)

### BANGALORE / PUNE (moderate)
- Widest material latitude: fabric sofas, cane, rattan all viable.
- Still: BWR+ plywood in kitchens; anti-fungal paint on exterior-facing walls (long damp spells).
- Monsoon rules apply Jun–Sep: matte over gloss, ventilation gaps in wardrobes, upholstery off exterior walls.

### MUMBAI / CHENNAI / KOLKATA (coastal humid)
- Cabinetry: BWP/marine-grade ply (IS 710) ONLY; MR ply delaminates in 5 years at this humidity.
- AVOID: particle board, cheap MDF, untreated solid wood, leather (mildew — AC rooms only),
  iron/mild-steel legs and hardware (salt air rust), velvet/silk/jute rugs (hold moisture, mold).
- RECOMMEND: stainless/powder-coated aluminium fittings, teak or sheesham (natural oils),
  engineered wood over solid flooring, linen/poly-blend quick-dry upholstery, sheer drapes.

### DELHI / NCR (45° summers, <5° winters, dust)
- Design for seasonal swap: light cotton/linen + reflective colors in summer; wool throws in winter.
- AVOID: open shelving, heavy-pile rugs/fabrics (dust traps). Prefer closed storage, washable
  covers, easy-wipe surfaces. Seasoned solid wood is fine; cheap wood cracks in winter dryness.

### HOT-DRY (Jaipur, interior MP/UP)
- Cotton breathes best; seating away from windows; MR ply is fine — don't over-spec BWP.

## Orientation rules (from window direction visible on camera or asked)
- North-facing: soft glare-free light — any color/fabric safe; best wall for a desk.
- West-facing: 3–6pm heat + fade — blackout/lined curtains, UV film; no silk/dark cotton near
  window; don't put the main sofa against the west window wall.
- East-facing: gentle morning sun — great for beds/breakfast; sheers suffice.
- Direct sun path on sofa/rug/art → warn about fading; fade-resistant fabrics, rotate rugs.
- AC: never bed/sofa directly under the blast. Ceiling fan: ~7ft clearance, no tall floor lamps under it.

## Vaastu (OPT-IN — ask "want me to keep Vaastu in mind, or skip it?" once, early)
The ~8 rules clients actually ask about:
1. Bed head to South or West; never head North; not under a beam.
2. Mirror never opposite the bed or facing the main door; dressing mirror on N/E wall.
3. Master bedroom SW; avoid NE (deity zone) and SE (fire zone) bedrooms.
4. Kitchen SE, cook facing East; stove and sink separated. NW second-best.
5. Pooja space NE, clutter-free, worshipper faces East, never adjacent to a toilet.
6. Entrance N/E/NE, well-lit, no mirror opposite, no shoe rack blocking.
7. Heavy furniture SW; keep NE corner and the home's center light and open.
8. Colors: living white/cream/light blue; kitchen yellow/orange; bedroom earthy pastels;
   no bright red in bedrooms, no near-black walls.
Frame remedies, not fear: "if the bed must face X, a curtain over the mirror is the common remedy."

## Structural / renter constraints
- RENTER → reversible only: peel-and-stick wallpaper, no-drill hooks, tension rods, freestanding
  furniture, rugs over floors, plug-in lighting. No repainting/drilling assumptions.
- OWNER (apartment) → load-bearing walls untouchable without municipal + society approval;
  partition changes need society NOC; balcony enclosure usually prohibited; wet-area relocation
  = high-risk (shared plumbing stacks, #1 society dispute).
- Dimensions: bedrooms 10×12 typical (double bed 5×6.5ft needs 10×12; king needs 12×12+);
  living 130–160 sqft; ceilings ~9.5ft, 8ft under false ceilings — check before pendants.
- Clearances: 2–3ft around bed, 3ft in front of wardrobes, 30–36" walkways, 14–18" sofa-to-coffee-table.
- Sofa >72–80" → warn: measure the service lift, stairwell turns, and 3ft main door. Prefer
  modular/sectional for rooms under 120 sqft.

## Budget bands (metro 2025, speak as ranges)
- Living room refresh: economy ₹1–2L (paint+curtains+rug+lighting+one anchor piece);
  mid ₹3–5L (sofa set, TV unit, false ceiling, soft furnishings); premium ₹7L+.
- Line items: false ceiling+lighting ₹100–250/sqft; laminate flooring ₹150–400/sqft;
  wardrobe ₹80k–3L; modular kitchen ₹1.5–6L. Materials ~60–70% of cost, labor ~20–30%; +10–15% contingency.
- SPEND on: sofa (₹40k+ frame), custom curtains, good paint, one statement light, mattress.
- SAVE on: cushions/linens, pouffes, lamps, art prints, accessories.
- Budget-constrained sequence: paint + lighting + curtains = ~80% of perceived refresh at ~20% of cost.

## Trend pack 2025–26 (cache at session start; cite source + year, max ONE trend per room)
- Asian Paints Colour of the Year 2026: "Moonlit Silk" — soft green, yellow/white undertones.
- Palette shift: sage, terracotta, mustard, indigo, warm whites — away from all-grey.
- Styles: warm minimalism, Japandi, quiet luxury, grandmillennial (cane+brass+heritage prints —
  maps to Indian ancestral furniture), artisan maximalism (handloom, block prints), curved furniture.
- Materials: cane/rattan, lime plaster, terracotta tile, bamboo ply, brushed metals, slatted acoustic panels.
- India-specific: climate-responsive as style (jaali screens, verandah revival), balconies as
  micro-rooms, Vaastu-integrated layouts as a selling point.
- OUT: all-grey, high-gloss gold, overdone boho. DON'T translate to India: wall-to-wall carpet,
  velvet-everything, solid-wood floors in coastal cities.
